Towards Robust Retrieval-Augmented Generation Based on Knowledge Graph: A Comparative Analysis

Authors: Hazem Amamou, Stéphane Gagnon, Alan Davoust and Anderson R. Avila

Source: https://arxiv.org/abs/arXiv:2603.05698

Generated summary artifact for 2603.05698

Thesis

Combining knowledge graph-based methods with Retrieval-Augmented Generation (RAG) models can significantly enhance LLM robustness towards noisy and inconsistent retrieved information.

Core Problem

There is an increasing concern with the impact of inconsistent retrieved information towards LLMs' responses, challenging the robustness of Retrieval-Augmented Generation (RAG) systems.

Key Novelty

Customizing GraphRAG, a knowledge-graph-based RAG system, to address four robustness challenges (noise robustness, information integration, negative rejection, counterfactual robustness) identified by the RGB benchmark.

Key Contributions

  • GraphRAG customizations significantly improve accuracy for counterfactual robustness (ACCdoc) by up to +85.94% for GPT-3.5 and +23.00% for error detection (ED*) for GPT-4o-mini compared to the RGB baseline.
  • Information integration accuracy improves by up to +31.86% for GPT-3.5 and +5.87% for GPT-4o-mini with customized GraphRAG at 0% noise.
  • GR_ext achieves the highest negative rejection rates, at 42.66% for GPT-4o-mini and 33% for GPT-3.5, demonstrating an improved ability to identify unanswerable questions.
  • GraphRAG augmentation provides greater benefits for less complex models like GPT-3.5, showing larger performance increases across tasks.

Results

  • Customized GraphRAG approaches significantly enhance LLM robustness to noise, counterfactual errors, and challenges in information integration compared to the standard RGB baseline.
  • The combination of encoded and external knowledge (GR_comb) and exclusive use of external knowledge (GR_ext) yield the greatest gains, with GR_comb achieving maximum error detection and correction, and GR_ext achieving maximum rejection scores.
  • Knowledge graph augmentation is particularly beneficial for models with limited encoded knowledge, such as GPT-3.5, showing greater performance increases than for GPT-4o-mini.
  • Current RAG systems still face challenges in negative rejection, with overall rejection rates remaining below 50%, indicating a need for further development in this area.

Limitations

  • Future research should include solutions based on KG as baselines.
  • A unified solution for all four tasks addressed in the RGB benchmark is desired, removing the need for task-specific GraphRAG customization.
  • Further refinement is needed for negative rejection approaches, potentially by integrating confidence scores for retrieved facts.
  • Exploration of multimodal RAG is a future direction.
  • Batch processing for larger document sets for efficiency in multi-query scenarios should be explored.
