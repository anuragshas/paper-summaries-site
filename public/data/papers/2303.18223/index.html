A Survey of Large Language Models

Authors: Wayne Xin Zhao, Kun Zhou, Junyi Li, Tianyi Tang, Xiaolei Wang, Yupeng Hou, Yingqian Min, Beichen Zhang, Junjie Zhang, Zican Dong, Yifan Du, Chen Yang, Yushuo Chen, Zhipeng Chen, Jinhao Jiang, Ruiyang Ren, Yifan Li, Xinyu Tang, Zikang Liu, Peiyu Liu, Jian-Yun Nie and Ji-Rong Wen

Thesis

This survey provides an up-to-date review of the recent advances in Large Language Models (LLMs), covering their background, key findings, and mainstream techniques across pre-training, adaptation tuning, utilization, and capacity evaluation, while also summarizing available resources and discussing future directions to serve as a useful resource for researchers and engineers.

Core Problem

Machines cannot naturally grasp the abilities of understanding and communicating in human language, which remains a longstanding research challenge for artificial intelligence.

Key Novelty

A comprehensive, up-to-date survey of Large Language Models (LLMs), distilling recent advances in pre-training, adaptation tuning, utilization, and capacity evaluation, alongside resources and future directions.

Key Contributions

  • LLMs exhibit surprising emergent abilities (e.g., in-context learning, step-by-step reasoning) not observed in smaller models, appearing when scale exceeds a certain level.
  • The launch of ChatGPT and GPT-4 showcased advanced conversational and multimodal capabilities, driving widespread attention to LLMs.
  • Scaling model size, data size, and training compute consistently leads to improved LLM capacity, with specific scaling laws (KM, Chinchilla) guiding optimal resource allocation.
  • Alignment tuning (e.g., RLHF) is crucial for aligning LLMs with human values and preferences, effectively mitigating the generation of toxic, fictitious, or harmful content.
  • Instruction tuning enhances LLMs' ability to follow task instructions and improves generalization to unseen tasks.

Results

  • Scaling laws demonstrate that increasing model size, data size, and training compute consistently improves LLM capacity and performance.
  • LLMs exhibit emergent abilities like in-context learning and step-by-step reasoning that are not present in smaller models and appear non-linearly with scale.
  • Effective data collection, rigorous preprocessing (filtering, deduplication, privacy reduction), and optimized data scheduling (mixture, curriculum) are paramount for building capable LLMs.
  • Various architectures beyond the vanilla Transformer, including Mixture-of-Experts and emergent SSM-based designs, aim to improve efficiency and scalability.
  • Adaptation techniques such as instruction tuning and alignment tuning (e.g., RLHF) are essential for enhancing task-specific performance and ensuring LLMs are helpful, honest, and harmless.

Limitations

  • The underlying principles behind emergent abilities in LLMs are still not well explored, lacking deep investigations into key contributing factors.
  • Training capable LLMs is extremely costly due to huge computational demands, making repetitive ablating studies difficult and limiting community access to important training details often confined to industry.
  • Aligning LLMs with human values or preferences is challenging, as LLMs are prone to generating toxic, fictitious, or harmful content, necessitating effective and efficient control approaches.
  • LLMs still suffer from limitations such as generating hallucinations with factual errors and potentially risky responses in specific contexts.
  • The capacities of LLMs are sometimes limited by their pre-training data, for example, an inability to capture up-to-date information, requiring external tools for compensation.
  • LLMs may perform less effectively on tasks not optimally expressed in text form, such as numerical computation.
