STATe-of-Thoughts: Structured Action Templates for Tree-of-Thoughts

Authors: Zachary Bamberger, Till R. Saenger, Gilad Morad, Ofra Amir, Brandon M. Stewart, Amir Feder

Source: https://arxiv.org/abs/2602.14265

Generated summary artifact for 2602.14265

Thesis

STATe-of-Thoughts (STATe) is an interpretable Inference-Time-Compute (ITC) framework that enhances output diversity, controllability, and quality in large language models by replacing stochastic sampling with discrete, action-guided textual interventions in a tree-search paradigm.

Core Problem

Existing Inference-Time-Compute (ITC) methods like Best-of-N and Tree-of-Thoughts (ToT) often fail to achieve meaningful output diversity due to reliance on high-temperature sampling and offer limited control over the reasoning process, which restricts explainability.

Key Novelty

A controllable and interpretable Inference-Time-Compute (ITC) framework, STATe-of-Thoughts, that leverages structured action templates for explicit search over high-level reasoning patterns within a Tree-of-Thoughts paradigm.

Key Contributions

  • STATe consistently achieves the highest semantic diversity across various models and temperatures on NoveltyBench, outperforming temperature-based stochastic sampling and other ITC methods, without degrading output quality.
  • The framework enables greater control and interpretability over generation, as demonstrated by the strong predictability of argument quality from sequential action features.
  • STATe's M2-guided trajectory selection successfully identifies and generates high-quality arguments from previously unobserved regions of the action space, substantially outperforming random and topic-presence baselines.
  • The level of coupling between reasoning actions and final output (synthesis mode) significantly impacts the predictability of argument quality.

Results

  • STATe-of-Thoughts significantly enhances output diversity compared to temperature-based sampling and other ITC methods without quality degradation, showing up to twice as many semantically distinct outputs on NoveltyBench.
  • Sequential action features, which capture the timing and order of controller decisions, are highly predictive of argument quality (e.g., R2 of 0.57 under strict synthesis), outperforming simpler presence-based models.
  • The attribution framework allows for targeted generation, enabling STATe to identify and explore promising, previously unseen regions of the action space, consistently outperforming random and topic-based baselines in argument quality win rates.

Limitations

  • STATe's reliance on prefilling text prefixes for intervention-based branching makes it currently straightforward primarily with open-source models, which may be less capable than advanced closed-source models.
  • The attribution analysis of action-outcome relationships is observational, not causal, meaning it identifies correlations rather than direct causal effects.
  • The rigidity in action and prefix design means actions are realized by fixed textual prefixes, which can limit natural surface forms and create stylistic artifacts if not carefully engineered.
  • There is a trade-off between control and quality in the synthesis step; strict synthesis offers high predictability but can produce stilted prose, while more flexible modes improve eloquence but reduce the direct mapping from actions to output properties.
  • The current approach to generating multiple realizations of the same trajectory through random seeds yields only limited diversity, suggesting a need for alternative variation methods at the tree level.
  • The framework currently focuses on single-turn, multi-step generation and does not explicitly model multi-turn conversational dynamics or support general tool calling beyond what is encoded in action templates.
