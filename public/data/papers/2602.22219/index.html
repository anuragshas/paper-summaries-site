Core Problem
Applying Retrieval-Augmented Generation (RAG) to structured knowledge graphs (KGs), especially in e-commerce, presents challenges in scaling retrieval across connected graphs, preserving contextual relationships during response generation, and effectively integrating cross-encoders with structured data.
Why it matters:
  • Conventional Large Language Models (LLMs) are prone to generating hallucinated or outdated content, especially when queries relate to proprietary or non-public information.
  • RAG addresses this limitation by grounding LLM responses in authoritative, domain-specific external knowledge, which is crucial for enterprise applications.
  • Production-level RAG systems for complex data structures like knowledge graphs require semantic reasoning capabilities, fast execution, and low computational costs to be scalable.
  • E-commerce platforms present unique challenges due to their scale, proprietary nature, complex structural organization of product catalogs, and diverse, complex customer queries.
Concrete Example:Processing natural language queries for product information from large-scale, proprietary e-commerce product catalogs (Knowledge Bases) with millions of products and complex relationships.
Key Novelty
Design and empirical evaluation of specialized Retriever-Reranker pipelines for Retrieval-Augmented Generation (RAG) over proprietary semi-structured knowledge bases (SKBs) in e-commerce, integrating dense/sparse retrieval with cross-encoder reranking.
  • The paper introduces and technically specifies three novel Retriever-Reranker pipeline configurations: FRWSR (FAISS Retrieval + Webis Set-encoder Reranking), FRMR (FAISS Retrieval + MS MARCO Reranking), and BARMR (BM25 Augmented Retrieval + MS MARCO Reranking). These pipelines integrate dense or sparse retrieval with advanced cross-encoder reranking specifically for knowledge graphs.
  • It conducts the first empirical performance evaluation of these specialized RAG pipelines on the Amazon STaRK benchmark, a production-scale e-commerce dataset that includes detailed product attributes and inter-product relationships. This evaluation uses ground-truth node responses, enabling direct comparison with published baselines.
  • The study provides actionable insights for optimizing RAG models that leverage Semi-structured Knowledge Base (SKB) architectures in natural language query processing. It demonstrates substantial performance improvements (e.g., 20.4% higher Hit@1 and 14.5% higher MRR) over existing benchmarks, informing the development of scalable, enterprise-grade RAG systems for domain-specific applications.
Evaluation Highlights
  • Achieved 20.4% higher Hit@1 and 14.5% higher Mean Reciprocal Rank (MRR) compared to published benchmarks on the Amazon STaRK dataset, demonstrating significant performance gains.
  • Empirically demonstrated that cross-encoder reranking provides statistically significant improvements over standalone retrieval methods, substantially boosting overall pipeline effectiveness.
  • Identified the FAISS HNSW + webis/set-encoder-large pipeline configuration as the best-performing model, excelling across all evaluation metrics.
  • Validated that careful model selection, optimized pre-processing, and multi-stage pipeline design are critical for building scalable, enterprise-grade RAG systems tailored for e-commerce.
Breakthrough Assessment
7.5/10
This paper makes a strong contribution by addressing a practical and challenging problem: Retrieval-Augmented Generation (RAG) over structured knowledge graphs in e-commerce. It provides a systematic evaluation of various retriever-reranker pipeline components and introduces specific well-engineered pipeline variants that significantly outperform existing benchmarks. The empirical validation on a large-scale, proprietary-like dataset (Amazon STaRK SKB) provides strong evidence of its utility and offers actionable insights for real-world deployments. While the core RAG components exist, the novel combination, optimization, and rigorous benchmarking for this specific, complex domain (SKB + cross-encoders) constitute a substantial and impactful step forward, moving beyond general RAG to domain-specific, production-ready systems.
