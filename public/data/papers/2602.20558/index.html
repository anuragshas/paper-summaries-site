Verbalization for LLM-based recommendation should be a learnable component, optimized end-to-end for recommendation accuracy through a data-centric, two-stage reinforcement learning framework that decouples context generation from preference reasoning, leading to significant performance gains and emergent effective verbalization strategies.
Core Problem
The core problem is the suboptimal verbalization of structured user interaction logs into natural language inputs for LLM-based recommendation systems. Existing methods rely on rigid templates that simply concatenate fields, yielding poor representations and hindering LLM reasoning.
Why it matters:
  • Rigid template-based verbalization creates suboptimal representations for recommendation.
  • Heterogeneous features in raw logs introduce parsing overhead and consume LLM model capacity.
  • Not all interactions carry equally important predictive signals, leading to irrelevant information being processed by LLMs.
  • The absence of semantic context in raw logs forces LLMs to reason purely from behavioral patterns, harming recommendation quality, especially for cold-start items.
Concrete Example:A typical user interaction log containing timestamps, device, item IDs, engagement types, and viewing durations, when presented verbatim to an LLM, introduces parsing overhead, lacks semantic context, and leads to suboptimal recommendations.
Key Novelty
Learning optimal verbalization for LLM-based recommendation using a data-centric, reinforcement learning framework, instead of relying on fixed templates or manual prompt engineering.
  • The paper introduces a novel data-centric framework that treats verbalization (converting structured logs into natural language) as a learnable component for LLM-based recommendation, moving beyond fixed templates or heuristic-based prompting.
  • It proposes a two-component architecture, the Verbalizer and the Reasoner, decoupling context optimization from preference reasoning.
  • A two-stage Group Relative Policy Optimization (GRPO)-based training framework is developed, enabling end-to-end optimization of both components without requiring explicit verbalization labels.
  • The framework integrates a length-based reward component to balance informativeness and conciseness, preventing degraded solutions from over-compression or verbosity.
Evaluation Highlights
  • The proposed framework achieved up to 93% relative improvement in novel item recommendation accuracy over template-based baselines on an industrial-scale streaming dataset.
  • Learned verbalization strategies, such as syntax normalization, noise filtering, and preference summarization, were qualitatively observed to be critical for effective LLM-based recommendation.
  • The two-stage training pipeline successfully optimized both the Verbalizer and Reasoner components, demonstrating a powerful synergy that significantly boosted performance.
Breakthrough Assessment
8.0/10
The paper addresses a fundamental and underexplored problem of verbalization in LLM-based recommendation. By framing verbalization as a learnable component and optimizing it end-to-end with reinforcement learning, it introduces a highly novel and impactful approach. The significant performance gains (up to 92.9% relative improvement) on an industrial dataset, coupled with insightful qualitative analysis of emergent verbalization strategies, demonstrate a strong contribution to the field. It shifts the paradigm from fixed prompt engineering to learned context construction, which is a key step towards more robust and effective LLM-powered recommendation systems.
