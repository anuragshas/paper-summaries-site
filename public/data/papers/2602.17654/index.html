📝 Paper Summary

E-commerce SearchInformation RetrievalSemantic SearchContrastive LearningDeep Learning for SearchGraded RelevanceHard Negative MiningLarge Language Models (LLMs) in Retrieval
We propose a two-stage “Mine and Refine” contrastive training framework that optimizes semantic text embeddings for graded relevance in multi-category e-commerce search, leading to improved retrieval relevance and business impact.
Core Problem
Optimizing multi-category e-commerce search retrieval to effectively handle graded relevance for long-tail and rare queries, ensuring scalability and adherence to product/policy constraints.
Why it matters:
  • Demands embeddings that generalize to long tail and rare queries.
  • Requires scalable supervision compatible with product and policy constraints.
  • Production systems benefit from clear separation of similarly rated items.
  • Delivers statistically significant gains in engagement and business impact.
Concrete Example:In e-commerce search, users might be satisfied with 'substitute' or 'complement' items (graded relevance) in addition to exact matches. Existing retrieval systems often struggle to distinguish these subtle degrees of relevance, especially for long-tail or rare queries, while maintaining scalability and adhering to business policies.
Key Novelty
A two-stage 'Mine and Refine' contrastive training framework for semantic text embeddings, specifically designed for optimizing graded relevance in e-commerce search, incorporating a multi-class circle loss and policy-aligned data augmentation.
  • **Two-stage 'Mine and Refine' Framework**: A novel approach combining two distinct training stages. Stage 1 ('Mine') uses a label-aware supervised contrastive objective to establish a robust global semantic space. Stage 2 ('Refine') refines this space by mining hard negatives and applying a multi-class extension of circle loss.
  • **Multi-class Circle Loss for Graded Relevance**: Extends the circle loss objective to explicitly handle three levels of graded relevance (d0 for exact match, d1 for substitute/complement, d2 for irrelevant) by dynamically optimizing margins and sharpening boundaries between these classes.
  • **Policy-Aligned LLM for Hard Negative Resummarization**: A lightweight LLM is finetuned to re-summarize mined hard negatives, aligning them with predefined policy guidelines to improve the quality of negative samples.
  • **Robustness via Additive Spelling Augmentation and Synthetic Query Generation**: Introduces novel data augmentation techniques to improve model robustness to diverse, noisy, and long-tail queries, making the embeddings more resilient and generalizable.
Evaluation Highlights
  • Achieved significant improvements in offline retrieval metrics (NDCG@10, Recall@10, Precision@10) across various benchmarks, with up to +4.28% delta on Recall@10 against a strong pretrained embedding model baseline.
  • Demonstrated positive and statistically significant relative lifts in online A/B tests: +2.5% for Add To Cart Rate (ATCR), +1.1% for Conversion Rate (CVR), and +0.7% for Gross Order Value (GOV).
  • Ablation studies confirm the effectiveness of the two-stage training, multi-class circle loss, item taxonomy enrichment, synthetic query enrichment, and spelling augmentation, each contributing to performance gains.
  • The method shows enhanced semantic separability between different relevance grades, as visualized by violin plots of similarity score distributions and average margins.
Breakthrough Assessment
7.5/10
The paper introduces a novel two-stage contrastive learning framework, 'Mine and Refine,' tailored for optimizing graded relevance in e-commerce search. It combines a label-aware supervised contrastive loss (SuPCon) for initial semantic space learning with a multi-class extension of circle loss for fine-grained boundary sharpening, along with innovative data augmentation. The framework demonstrates significant improvements in both offline evaluation metrics (NDCG, Recall, Precision) and critical online business metrics (Add To Cart Rate, Conversion Rate, Gross Order Value) in a real-world production setting, representing a strong practical and methodological contribution to semantic search and contrastive learning.
