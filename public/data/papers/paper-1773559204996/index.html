Think before Recommendation: Autonomous Reasoning-enhanced Recommender

Authors: Xiaoyu Kong, Junguang Jiang, Bin Liu, Ziru Xu, Han Zhu, Jian Xu, Bo Zheng, Jiancan Wu, Xiang Wang

Source: https://openreview.net/pdf?id=fVs2BCjCqC

Generated summary artifact for paper-1773559204996

Thesis

This paper proposes RecZero, an RL-based recommendation paradigm that trains a single LLM to autonomously develop reasoning capabilities for rating prediction, overcoming limitations of traditional distillation-based methods such as insufficient recommendation capability, costly supervision, and superficial transfer of reasoning ability. It also introduces RecOne, a hybrid paradigm combining SFT with RL for faster convergence and stronger performance.

Core Problem

Existing distillation-based methods for leveraging LLMs' reasoning capabilities in rating prediction suffer from limitations such as insufficient recommendation capability of teacher models, costly and static supervision, and superficial transfer of reasoning ability.

Key Novelty

A novel RL-based paradigm (RecZero) for LLM-enhanced recommendation that enables autonomous reasoning and eliminates multi-model, multi-stage distillation, complemented by a hybrid approach (RecOne) for robust performance.

Key Contributions

  • RecOne achieved significant performance improvements, lowering RMSE by 6.7% on Book, 12.2% on Music, and 6.2% on Yelp, and cutting MAE by 16.8% on Book, 29.9% on Music, and 7.5% on Yelp, compared to the strongest LLM-based baseline (Reason4Rec).
  • RecZero (pure RL) also surpassed all baselines in terms of MAE across the three datasets, demonstrating the effectiveness of the RL paradigm even without cold-start initialization.
  • RecZero(early-stop) achieved a 21.5% MAE reduction against the strongest pure-SFT baseline (Reason4Rec) with significantly fewer labeled samples (0.48K vs. 20K) and computational resources, demonstrating superior cost-effectiveness.
  • The cold-start SFT initialization in RecOne proved effective in enhancing the model's initial capabilities and elevating its performance ceiling for RL optimization, achieving faster convergence.
  • Ablation studies confirmed the critical impact of proposed components such as structured multi-step thinking and rule-based reward modeling on recommendation performance.

Results

  • RecOne significantly outperforms all baseline methods, including traditional and LLM-based approaches, across all evaluated metrics (RMSE and MAE) and datasets.
  • The pure RL approach (RecZero) also surpasses all baselines in terms of MAE, demonstrating the inherent superiority of the RL paradigm for autonomous reasoning-enhanced recommendation.
  • RecOne's cold-start supervised fine-tuning (SFT) initialization effectively boosts the model's initial capabilities and elevates its performance ceiling during subsequent RL optimization.
  • The proposed components, including the structured 'Think-before-Recommendation' prompt and rule-based reward modeling, are crucial for achieving the superior performance of RecOne.
  • RecZero and RecOne offer a simpler and more cost-efficient pipeline (single model, teacher-free, end-to-end optimization) compared to complex multi-stage distillation methods, requiring fewer labeled samples, less training time, and lower inference cost.
  • Both RecZero and RecOne enable fast adaptation to cold-start scenarios and distribution shifts with minimal new interactions, making them well-suited for dynamic commercial recommender systems.

Limitations

  • Due to computational constraints, the study could not fully assess the potential performance gains achievable by leveraging larger base models for RL, potentially hindering a thorough evaluation of the models' true performance potential.
  • The study did not explore whether RecZero and RecOne could serve as viable replacements for existing Teacher models in generating cold-start data for multi-round self-iterative optimization.
