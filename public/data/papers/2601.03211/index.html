Fine-Tuning Small Language Models as Efficient Enterprise Search Relevance Labelers

Authors: Yue Kang, Zhuoyi Huang, Benji Schussheim, Diana Licon, Dina Atia, Shixing Cao, Jacob Danovitch, Kunho Kim, Billy Norcilien, Jonah Karpman, Mahmound Sayed, Mike Taylor, Tao Sun, Pavel Metrikov, Vipul Agarwal, Chris Quirk, Ye-Yi Wang, Nick Craswell, Irene Shaffer, Tianwei Chen, Sulaiman Vesal, Soundar Srinivasan

Source: https://arxiv.org/abs/2601.03211

Thesis

To overcome the challenges of data scarcity and high labeling costs in enterprise search, fine-tuning Small Language Models (SLMs) with a novel synthetic data generation pipeline can achieve relevance labeling quality comparable to state-of-the-art Large Language Models (LLMs) while drastically improving throughput and cost-efficiency.

Core Problem

In enterprise search, building high-quality datasets at scale remains a central challenge due to the difficulty of acquiring labeled data, exacerbated by the lack of publicly available, high-quality enterprise search datasets and the computational expense and limited throughput of Large Language Models (LLMs) for large-scale labeling tasks.

Key Novelty

An efficient and scalable synthetic data generation and SLM fine-tuning pipeline for enterprise search relevance labeling, achieving LLM-comparable performance with significant throughput and cost advantages.

Key Contributions

  • Achieved SLM-Human NDCG of 0.953 and pairwise accuracy of 63.81, outperforming GPT-4o (NDCG: 0.944, Accuracy: 62.58) on the enterprise search test dataset.
  • Demonstrated a throughput of 873.33 RPM on a single A100 GPU, extrapolating to nearly 7K RPM on a cluster, which is an order of magnitude faster than typical LLM labelers.
  • Achieved significant cost efficiency, being nearly 19 times cheaper than GPT-4o for both input tokens ($0.13 vs $2.50 per 1M) and output tokens ($0.52 vs $10.00 per 1M).
  • Statistical significance tests (Wilcoxon signed-rank test) confirmed that the fine-tuned labeler is non-inferior to the GPT-4o baseline within specified small margins for both pairwise accuracy and NDCG.
  • Qualitative analysis showed improved calibration and confidence in assigning lowest relevance scores for irrelevant passages compared to vanilla SLMs and GPT-4o.

Results

  • The fine-tuned Small Language Model (Phi-3.5 Mini Instruct) achieves performance comparable to or even surpassing GPT-4o on enterprise relevance labeling, demonstrating strong alignment with human judgments.
  • The proposed synthetic data generation pipeline, which includes LLM-based query generation, BM25-based negative mining, and LLM-assisted labeling, is highly effective in producing high-quality training data for enterprise search.
  • Fine-tuned SLMs offer substantial improvements in throughput (17x faster) and cost-efficiency (19x cheaper) compared to LLMs for large-scale relevance labeling, making them practical for real-world deployments.
  • Ablation studies confirm the critical importance of multi-task tuning and training data quality (e.g., query refinement) over simply increasing the raw quantity of synthetic data beyond a certain threshold.

Limitations

  • An initial approach to fine-tune SLMs as query generators struggled to produce high-quality, diverse, and keyword-based queries due to positivity bias in pretraining and limited model capacity, necessitating a different strategy.
  • The experiments primarily utilized Phi-3.5 Mini Instruct and GPT-4o; the paper notes that results with newer models (e.g., GPT-5 or other SLM families) were not included, though the authors believe the core findings generalize.
