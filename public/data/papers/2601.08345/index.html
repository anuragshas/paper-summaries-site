This paper proposes MLPlatt, a simple yet effective context-aware calibration framework for ranking models, which transforms uncalibrated ranker outputs into interpretable and well-calibrated Click-Through Rate (CTR) probabilities while preserving the original item ordering.
Core Problem
Ranking models often suffer from poor interpretability and non-scale calibration, especially when utilizing ranking loss functions that prioritize order over absolute probability. This limits their applicability for post-hoc calibration and direct probabilistic interpretation, which are crucial for many real-world applications.
Why it matters:
  • Uncalibrated ranker outputs are intrinsically uninterpretable as true probabilities, hindering their use in decision-making.
  • Many downstream tasks, such as ad ranking, bid strategies, and engagement prediction, require accurate probabilistic outputs.
  • The lack of calibration often necessitates separate Click-Through Rate (CTR) estimators, increasing system complexity and potential inconsistencies.
  • Probabilistic interpretations are essential for transparent and consistent integration of diverse signals (e.g., relevance, sponsored content) to balance user experience and platform revenue.
Concrete Example:In e-commerce, a ranking model might output scores that accurately order items, but these scores don't directly represent click probabilities. This makes it challenging to use them effectively for downstream tasks like bid optimization in ad ranking or comparing the value of sponsored versus organic search results, where true probabilities are required.
Key Novelty
A context-aware and monotonic calibration framework (MLPlatt) for converting uncalibrated ranking model scores into interpretable Click-Through Rate (CTR) probabilities, while preserving the original ranking order.
  • MLPlatt introduces a Multi-Layer Platt architecture that transforms raw, uncalibrated ranker scores into well-calibrated Click-Through Rate (CTR) probabilities.
  • A key innovation is the integration of listing-level contextual features (xctx) into the calibration process. This 'context awareness' allows MLPlatt to perform field-level calibration, ensuring accurate probabilities across different user-device-country strata, which traditional post-hoc methods often fail to achieve.
  • The framework includes a novel monotonicity constraint that ensures the calibrated output (c) is monotonically increasing with respect to the original ranker score (r). This guarantees that the original ranking order is preserved while providing interpretable probabilities.
  • It is designed to be lightweight and easily integrable into existing ranking pipelines, acting as a post-hoc calibration layer without significantly increasing latency.
Evaluation Highlights
  • MLPlatt achieved over a 10x reduction in Field-level Expected Calibration Error (F-ECE) on the proprietary Allegro dataset (0.0277 to 0.0021), demonstrating significant improvement in calibration quality.
  • The method consistently outperformed state-of-the-art post-hoc calibration techniques (Platt Scaling, Isotonic Regression, ConfCalib, DESC) across two large-scale e-commerce datasets (Allegro and AliExpress) in terms of F-ECE and LogLoss.
  • MLPlatt improved Area Under the Curve (AUC) by ~12% on Allegro and ~1% on AliExpress, showing that calibration can be achieved without harming, and even improving, ranking performance.
  • The monotonicity constraint successfully preserved the original ranking order, ensuring that calibration does not negatively impact the relative ordering of items.
  • Ablation studies confirmed the crucial role of both the Context Model and the Monotonic Multi-Layer Perceptron (MonoMLP) components for achieving high-quality context-aware calibration.
Breakthrough Assessment
7.5/10
This paper presents a strong contribution to the field of ranking model calibration, particularly in e-commerce. The key novelty lies in the context-aware and monotonic Multi-Layer Platt architecture, which significantly improves calibration quality (F-ECE, LogLoss) while preserving ranking order and even boosting ranking performance (AUC). The ability to convert uncalibrated ranker scores into interpretable CTR probabilities, leveraging granular contextual features, is highly valuable for real-world applications (e.g., ad ranking, bidding strategies). The method is robust and demonstrated superior performance on large-scale datasets compared to several state-of-the-art baselines. It addresses a practical and important problem, providing a clear path to integrate rankers into probabilistic decision-making pipelines.
