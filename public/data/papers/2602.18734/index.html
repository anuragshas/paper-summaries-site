By reformulating Retrieval-Augmented Generation (RAG) as a cooperative multi-agent decision-making problem and jointly optimizing the reranker and generator under a shared task-oriented reward, CoRAG mitigates the generator's asymmetric dependency on reranking results, leading to improved generation stability and generalization.
Core Problem
Many existing Retrieval-Augmented Generation (RAG) systems operate under a ranking-centric, asymmetric dependency where the generator's quality is highly sensitive to the reranker's output. This creates a mismatch: precisely ranking documents is difficult, yet the generator heavily relies on fine-grained ranking, leading to instability and poor generalization.
Why it matters:
  • The generator is highly sensitive to fine-grained ranking results from the reranker.
  • Learning an exact total order over multiple highly relevant documents is inherently harder than learning a relaxed ordering for the reranker.
  • This discrepancy between reranking optimization difficulty and generator sensitivity poses significant challenges for the stability and generalization of RAG frameworks in practice.

Key Novelty
Reformulating Retrieval-Augmented Generation (RAG) as a cooperative multi-agent decision-making problem with joint optimization under a shared task-oriented reward.
  • CoRAG reformulates RAG as a cooperative multi-agent decision-making problem, treating the reranker and generator as peer agents rather than being connected through an asymmetric dependency pipeline.
  • It proposes a joint optimization scheme where both the reranker and generator are optimized towards a shared, task-oriented reward, encouraging cooperation and mitigating the generator's reliance on overly strict document ordering.
  • To optimize the reranker, task-oriented rewards are transformed into document-level stochastic preference signals, which are then used with a GRPO-aligned framework and a margin-based pairwise ranking surrogate to provide stable and efficient optimization.
Evaluation Highlights
  • CoRAG achieved state-of-the-art results on four core QA benchmarks: PopQA, TriviaQA, NQ, and 2WikiMultiHopQA.
  • Demonstrated strong generalization ability, outperforming baselines on datasets it was not trained on (trained only on PopQA).
  • Showed robustness to varying numbers of retrieved documents (top-K analysis), unlike some baselines whose performance declined with more documents.
  • Maintained competitive performance on cross-domain tasks like code generation (HumanEval, HumanEval+) and table question answering (WTQ).
  • Consistently preferred by LLM-as-a-judge evaluators across multiple LLM judges, indicating robust and high-quality generation.
Breakthrough Assessment
7.5/10
The paper presents a strong contribution by reframing Retrieval-Augmented Generation (RAG) as a cooperative multi-agent decision-making problem, moving beyond the traditional asymmetric dependency. The proposed Cooperative RAG (CoRAG) framework, with its novel joint optimization strategy using a shared task-oriented reward, significantly improves generation stability and generalization. The experimental results consistently demonstrate state-of-the-art performance across multiple benchmarks, even when trained on limited data, and show improved robustness. While not a field-shifting paradigm, it offers a principled and effective solution to a critical issue in RAG system design.
