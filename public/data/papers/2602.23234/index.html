📝 Paper Summary

Information RetrievalLearning to RankLarge Language ModelsMulti-Objective OptimizationApp Store SearchRelevance Labeling
Large Language Models, when fine-tuned on human judgments and used as scalable offline label generators, can effectively overcome data scarcity for textual relevance. Integrating these LLM-generated labels into a multi-objective ranker's training pipeline fundamentally shifts the behavioral-textual relevance Pareto frontier outwards, leading to superior performance in both offline NDCG metrics and statistically significant online conversion rate improvements, particularly for tail queries in a real-world commercial setting like the App Store.
Core Problem
The core problem is the scarcity of high-quality, expert-provided textual relevance labels, which are crucial for effective search ranking systems that optimize for both textual and behavioral relevance. This scarcity leads to a scalability bottleneck and under-powers the textual relevance objective in multi-objective training.
Why it matters:
  • It creates a fundamental scalability bottleneck for large-scale commercial search systems.
  • It leaves the textual relevance objective under-powered in multi-objective learning-to-rank frameworks.
  • It limits the effectiveness of search ranking systems in helping users discover and engage with relevant applications, particularly for tail queries where behavioral data is sparse.
Concrete Example:An App Store search system needs to rank apps, balancing semantic fit (textual relevance) and likelihood of download/click (behavioral relevance). Obtaining sufficient expert human judgments for textual relevance for millions of query-app pairs is impractical and creates a scalability bottleneck.
Key Novelty
A scalable industrial strategy for augmenting multi-objective search ranker training data using millions of high-quality textual relevance labels generated by a fine-tuned Large Language Model (LLM) acting as an offline judge, leading to significant improvements in both offline and online ranking performance, particularly for tail queries.
  • The paper systematically evaluates various LLM configurations, including pretrained and fine-tuned models with different parameter counts (3B vs 30B), and prompt engineering techniques (zero-shot vs. few-shot, textual vs. numeric labels). This rigorous evaluation identifies an optimal fine-tuned, smaller LLM (FT-3B) that significantly outperforms much larger pretrained models for generating high-quality relevance labels.
  • Leveraging the optimal LLM configuration, the authors generate millions of pointwise textual relevance labels offline across multiple languages. This approach acts as a 'force multiplier' for human annotation, effectively overcoming data scarcity for textual relevance.
  • The LLM-generated labels are integrated into the training pipeline of a multi-objective production ranker. This augmentation leads to a significant outward shift of the behavioral-textual relevance Pareto frontier, meaning the ranker simultaneously improves both textual and behavioral NDCG metrics, strictly dominating previous production models.
  • The efficacy of the approach is validated through a comprehensive evaluation, including offline NDCG metrics and a large-scale online A/B test. The A/B test demonstrates a statistically significant +0.24% increase in conversion rate in a real-world commercial setting, with particular impact on tail queries where behavioral data is scarce.
Evaluation Highlights
  • A fine-tuned 3B LLM (FT-3B) significantly outperformed pretrained models (PT-3B, PT-30B) in textual relevance label generation, achieving an F1 score of 0.800 compared to 0.382 for the best pretrained model (PT-30B).
  • Augmenting the production ranker with LLM-generated labels led to a simultaneous improvement in both textual and behavioral NDCG, demonstrating a true Pareto improvement (e.g., Textual NDCG@1 increased from 0.867 to 0.868, Behavioral NDCG@1 from 0.646 to 0.652).
  • A worldwide A/B test on the App Store ranker showed a statistically significant +0.24% increase in conversion rate for the LLM-augmented model.
  • The most substantial online gains in conversion rate were observed for tail queries, where LLM-generated textual labels provided a robust and accurate signal in the absence of reliable behavioral data.
Breakthrough Assessment
7.5/10
This paper presents a strong industrial-scale validation of using fine-tuned LLMs as offline label generators to augment search ranking systems. It demonstrates significant and measurable improvements in both offline and online metrics, notably a +0.24% conversion rate increase in a worldwide App Store A/B test, especially for challenging tail queries. While the 'LLM-as-a-Judge' concept exists, its deployment at this scale, the systematic evaluation of LLM configurations, and the clear demonstration of shifting the Pareto frontier for multi-objective ranking make this a substantial practical and empirical contribution.
