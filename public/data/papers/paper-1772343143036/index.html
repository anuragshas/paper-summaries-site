Core Problem
End-to-end (E2E) audio language models (AudioLMs) struggle to maintain dialogue coherence, specifically in context maintenance and topic tracking, in complex, multi-turn task-oriented dialogues.
Why it matters:
  • Audio interfaces are crucial for accessibility for users with motor disabilities, visual impairments, low literacy, or situational constraints, making robust spoken dialogue systems essential.
  • AudioLMs promise low-latency, real-time audio interaction (200-320ms vs. 2-5s for modular systems), which is critical for natural conversational flow.
  • The inability of E2E models to maintain coherence and context hinders their practical applicability in real-world multi-turn task-oriented dialogue systems, despite their latency advantages.
Concrete Example:In multi-phase sales-customer scenarios, E2E audio language models like Qwen-Omni-3B (E2E) show severe degradation in naturalness, with scores plummeting from 51.4 to 23.9 within just seven turns, a problem not observed in modular counterparts.
Key Novelty
The first systematic investigation into the conversational performance of end-to-end audio language models (AudioLMs) versus modular ASR-LLM-TTS systems in multi-turn and multi-phase task-oriented dialogues, revealing a 'context trap' due to AudioLMs' fundamental limitations in dialogue modeling.
  • This is the first systematic and comprehensive investigation into the conversational performance of end-to-end audio language models compared to modular systems within the challenging domain of multi-turn and multi-phase task-oriented dialogues.
  • The study explicitly disentangles the impact of audio generation quality from core dialogue modeling capabilities on overall performance, demonstrating that audio quality is not the bottleneck.
  • It provides a detailed error analysis, categorizing and quantifying specific failure types that lead to dialogue degradation in E2E systems, identifying a "context trap" where these models struggle with coherence over turns.
Evaluation Highlights
  • First systematic investigation comparing E2E AudioLMs against modular (ASR+LLM+TTS) systems for multi-turn and multi-phase task-oriented dialogues.
  • Demonstrated that E2E AudioLMs consistently underperform modular systems, exhibiting severe turn-by-turn degradation in dialogue naturalness and consistency.
  • Revealed that dialogue quality degradation in E2E models is primarily due to fundamental limitations in dialogue modeling (context maintenance, topic tracking), not audio generation quality.
  • Quantified specific failure modes in E2E systems, showing significantly higher severity for Generic responses, Repetition, Memory errors, Misunderstanding, and Topic Drift compared to modular baselines.
Breakthrough Assessment
6.5/10
The paper provides a valuable, systematic investigation and clear empirical evidence of a critical weakness in current end-to-end audio language models for complex dialogue tasks. While it does not propose a new model or method, its detailed analysis and robust evaluation framework identify a significant "context trap" problem, offering crucial insights for future research directions in developing more robust AudioLMs. The finding that audio quality is *not* the bottleneck but dialogue modeling capabilities are, is a strong contribution to the understanding of these systems.
