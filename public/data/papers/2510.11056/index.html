From Reasoning LLMs to BERT: A Two-Stage Distillation Framework for Search Relevance

Authors: Runze Xia, Yupeng Ji, Yuxi Zhou, Haodong Liu, Teng Zhang, and Piji Li

Source: https://arxiv.org/abs/arXiv:2510.11056

Generated summary artifact for 2510.11056

Thesis

To overcome the limitations of direct LLM application in e-commerce search due to latency and domain-specificity, we propose a two-stage reasoning distillation framework that builds a powerful, domain-adapted teacher LLM and then efficiently transfers its reasoning capabilities to a lightweight BERT student model, achieving both high accuracy and inference efficiency.

Core Problem

E-commerce search systems require highly accurate and low-latency query-service relevance prediction. While Large Language Models (LLMs) offer deep reasoning capabilities, their high computational cost and latency prevent direct application in production. Conversely, lightweight models like BERT are efficient but lack deep reasoning and domain-specific knowledge.

Key Novelty

A two-stage reasoning distillation framework that transfers LLM reasoning capabilities to a lightweight BERT model for efficient and accurate e-commerce search relevance prediction, bridging the gap between LLM performance and BERT efficiency.

Key Contributions

  • General-purpose LLMs like Qwen3-8B and DeepSeek-R1 showed significantly lower performance (Macro F1 < 0.5) on domain-specific e-commerce search tasks compared to specialized models.
  • The proposed domain-adapted teacher LLM (CPT+SFT+GRPO(Weighted)) achieved a Macro F1 of 0.7174, demonstrating progressive improvement with each training stage (CPT, SFT, multi-dimensional reward-guided GRPO).
  • The Contrastive Reasoning Self-Distillation (CRSD Full) model achieved a Macro F1 of 0.7076, significantly outperforming a standard data distillation baseline (0.6945) and a baseline reasoning distillation method (0.6959), while retaining 98.6% of the teacher LLM's performance.
  • Ablation studies confirmed the critical importance of reasoning paths; removing them or injecting random paths actively degraded performance.
  • Online A/B testing in Meituan's search advertising system showed substantial relative improvements: +0.91% in AdCTR, +1.06% in AdCVR, and +0.40% in GTV.
  • Manual expert review revealed a 30.5 percentage point reduction in the bad case rate in the top 5 search results, confirming practical value and successful deployment.

Results

  • General-purpose LLMs demonstrate significantly lower performance on domain-specific e-commerce search tasks, underscoring the necessity of domain adaptation.
  • The multi-dimensional weighted reward model and GRPO optimization are crucial for refining the teacher LLM's reasoning capabilities, leading to progressive performance gains.
  • The Contrastive Reasoning Self-Distillation (CRSD) framework effectively transfers reasoning capabilities to a lightweight student model, achieving substantial improvements over baseline distillation methods.
  • The semantic content and relevance of reasoning paths are vital; their absence or noise actively degrades model performance.
  • Online A/B tests confirm that the proposed framework delivers significant improvements in key business metrics and reduces bad case rates, validating its practical value and deployability in a real-world setting.

Limitations

  • While the framework aims to minimize performance drop when distilling from a massive LLM to a small student, some degradation is inherent in the distillation process.
